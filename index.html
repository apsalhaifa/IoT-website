About the Department

The Department of Computer Science and Engineering (Internet of Things) is a vibrant and dynamic community of scholars, researchers, and students. Our department is committed to providing a world-class education in computer science and engineering, with a focus on the Internet of Things (IoT). We aim to equip students with the knowledge, skills, and expertise needed to succeed in this rapidly evolving field.

Our Goals

1. Produce Competent Professionals: We aim to produce graduates who are well-versed in IoT technologies and can apply their knowledge to real-world problems.
2. Foster Innovation and Entrepreneurship: We encourage students to think creatively and develop innovative solutions to IoT-related challenges. We also provide resources and support to help students turn their ideas into successful startups.
3. Establish Strong Industry-Academia Collaboration: We believe that collaboration between industry and academia is essential for advancing IoT research and development. We work closely with industry partners to provide students with hands-on experience and to develop solutions that address real-world challenges.
4. Promote Research and Development: We are committed to advancing the state-of-the-art in IoT research and development. Our faculty and students work on a wide range of IoT-related projects, from sensor networks and data analytics to cybersecurity and artificial intelligence.

Vision & Mission

Vision

To promote excellence in producing competent IoT professionals to serve society through technology and research.

Mission

  • Producing competent professionals in information and communication technologies.
  • Educating students with state-of-the-art computing environments and pedagogical innovations.
  • Encouraging entrepreneurship and imparting skills for employability.
  • Establishing collaboration with IoT and allied industries.
  • Promoting research in information and communication technology to improve the quality of human life.
  • Offering beneficial service to society by imparting knowledge and providing IoT solutions.

PEO, PSO & Program Outcomes

Program Educational Objectives (PEO):

  • Exhibit proficiency in analyzing, designing, and developing IoT-based solutions to cater to the needs of the industry. (Technical Competence)
  • Provide professional expertise to the industry and society with effective communication and ethics. (Professionalism)
  • Engage in lifelong learning for professional development and research. (Life-Long Learning)

Program Specific Outcomes (PSO):

  • PSO1: Design IoT-based software solutions using programming skills and computing technologies.
  • PSO2: Design and implement data communication systems using various IoT components.

Program Outcomes:

  • Apply the knowledge of mathematics, basic sciences, engineering fundamentals, and Computer Science and Engineering to solve complex engineering problems. (Engineering Knowledge)
  • Identify, formulate, review research literature, and analyze complex engineering problems requiring computing solutions. (Problem Analysis)
  • Design solutions for complex engineering problems and system components or processes, considering public health, safety, and environmental needs. (Design/Development of Solutions)
  • Use research-based knowledge and methods, including experimentation and data analysis, to provide valid conclusions. (Investigations of Complex Problems)
  • Create, select, and apply appropriate techniques and modern IoT tools to engineering activities while understanding their limitations. (Modern Tool Usage)
  • Assess societal, health, safety, legal, and cultural issues relevant to engineering practice. (The Engineer and Society)
  • Understand the impact of engineering solutions in societal and environmental contexts, demonstrating the need for sustainable development. (Environment and Sustainability)
  • Apply ethical principles and commit to professional ethics and responsibilities. (Ethics)
  • Function effectively as an individual and as a member or leader in diverse teams. (Individual and Teamwork)
  • Communicate effectively with both the engineering community and society at large. (Communication)
  • Apply engineering and management principles to work in team settings and manage cost-effective projects. (Project Management and Finance)
  • Recognize the need for independent and lifelong learning in a rapidly evolving technological landscape. (Life-long Learning)
